EPLEY e1RM FORMULA
e1RM = weight × (1 + reps ÷ 30)
Rounded to nearest 2.5 lb. Epley (1990) — accurate for 1–10 rep ranges. Pull-ups use bodyweight + added weight as the load input.

Training rules

How the app decides what to prescribe — and what it won't let you do.

Hard rules — enforced in code

These run before every AI call. The AI cannot override them. They protect against injury, overreaching, and training in a physiologically unsafe state.

Soft rules — AI judgment within bounds

These are evidence-based guidelines the AI follows when prescribing. They can be adjusted by context — your history, block phase, and recovery state all influence how the AI applies them.

Progressive overload
Double progression: reps first, then load. Add weight only when all sets reach the top of the rep range at RPE ≤8.
Rep ranges
Deadlift 3–5 · Squat 4–6 · Bench/OHP 5–8 · Row/Pullup 6–10. Weighted pullups 4–6.
Load increments
Lower body: +5lb when earned. Upper body: +2.5lb. Pullup belt: +5lb.
Stall detection
2 consecutive sessions below rep range minimum → auto-deload to 90% of working weight.
Zone 2 pacing
All easy runs at HR ≤147 bpm (MAF formula: 180 − 28 − 5). Current easy runs at 165–174 are threshold, not aerobic.
80/20 polarization
~80% of weekly run volume at easy/Zone 2 pace. ~20% at quality intensity. Source: Seiler (2009).
4-week block structure
Week 1: accumulate (baseline volume). Week 2: build (+33% sets). Week 3: peak (+67% sets). Week 4: deload (−40% volume, no quality).
Volume ceilings
Resident-adjusted maximum recoverable volume: Squat 16 sets/wk · Deadlift 10 · Bench 18 · OHP 14 · Row 16 · Pullup 16.
Run volume
Maximum 10% weekly mileage increase. Long run ≤35% of weekly total. Deload week: 50% of peak mileage.
Recovery windows
Quality → quality: 48h min. Long run → quality: 72h. Legs → quality run: 24h. Legs → long run: 48h.
What AI contributes
Weekly prescription
Sets exact weights, sets, reps, and RPE targets for all 6 lifts based on your session history and biometric state.
Run prescription
Prescribes target HR, pace, duration, and structure for each of your 3 weekly runs based on fitness trend.
Accessory selection
Chooses hypertrophy and stability accessories for each day based on block phase and your weak points.
Coaching narrative
Explains its reasoning — what it noticed in your data, what to watch for, and why it prescribed what it did.
Auto-regulation
Provides a specific rule for how to adjust the session in real time based on how it feels (e.g. stop set if RPE hits 9 early).
Recovery flag
Raises a warning when biometrics indicate significant under-recovery, even if no single hard rule fires.
When to do what
Generate AI Plan
After logging a session. Monday morning. After any rest period of 5+ days. When biometrics spike (resting HR +10 bpm or HRV drops 30%+). Not more than once per day.
Log a session
Immediately after training while details are fresh. Log RPE honestly — it's the primary signal the AI uses for progressive overload. One session per day type.
Daily check-in
Every morning before training. Takes 30 seconds. Energy, sleep, and post-call status are the most important inputs — they directly control what the hard rule engine allows today.
Mark post-call
Any morning after a call shift or night of total sleep loss. This is a hard block — the app will prevent quality and heavy training regardless of how you feel (catecholamine false readiness).
Override schedule
When life forces you to do a different workout than scheduled. Use the Today's Workout dropdown in Activity tab. The schedule ripples forward automatically.
When to rest
When the hard rule engine flags POST_CALL, FEVER_SIGNAL, or SLEEP_BLOCK. When HRV drops >20% below your 7-day mean. When you've had 3+ stalled lifts in 14 days (systemic overreaching signal).
